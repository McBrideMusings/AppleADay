Lode Runner

In Lode Runner, you are an intergalactic commando charged with recovering gold stashed away in 150 different treasure chambers put there by the deranged leadership of the Bungeling Empire. The leadership of the Empire got the gold by swindling their people out of it via fast-food taxes. You have tools allowing you to dig and drill through bricks for the gold. Once all the gold on a level has been collected, a ladder allowing you to move onto the next screen is added.

Impact
Lode Runner was one of the earliest games to include a level editor, allowing the creation of new level designs with no programming skill. There are ten types of building blocks to use in creating the screens including trap doors and monkey bars. Softline’s July 1983 issue describes Lode Runner as addicting and a ton of fun.

Production History
Lode Runner was created by Doug Smith in 1983. It was published by Brøderbund Software. Brøderbund, headed by Doug Carlston - who was one a member of the “brotherhood” of game makers in the early 1980s - was purchased by The Learning Company in 1998. However, the brand can still be seen on some original Brøderbund products.
