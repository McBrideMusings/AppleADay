Dino Eggs

In Dino Eggs, you are on an expedition through time to the prehistoric past. You must collect and bring dinosaur eggs to the future in order to save the race from extinction. The mothers of the eggs also lurk by, and will thwart your effort. Building a fire warns off the mothers.

Impact
Softline describes Dino Eggs as a rich game that will “certainly become a cult classic” in its September 1983 issue. It also describes the game as a moving tribute to the cinema classic, Bambi Meets Godzilla.

Production History
Dino Eggs was created in 1983. It was created by David Schroeder. Dino Eggs was published by Micro Fun, which stopped making games after 1984 - a potential victim of the “Shakeout”. During the Shakeout in the mid-1980s, games failed because of the failure of companies that made the platforms for the games. Microcomputers and consoles like the Commodore VIC and Atari stopped being made and game makers were left with unsellable games and losses that could not be recouped from the manufacturing of games created for the defunct consoles.
