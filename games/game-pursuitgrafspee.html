Pursuit of the Graf Spee

Described as a “dandy computer war game” in the Softline July 1982 issue, Pursuit of the Graf Spee is based on the Allied sinking of the German battleship Graf Spee early in World War II. You play a British commander with two aircraft carriers, four heavy cruisers, five light cruisers, and a battle cruiser that you will use to pursue and sink the German battleship. Each ship on the map is represented by a single letter, such as G for the Graf Spee.

Impact
Softline described the game as better and more fun to play than Strategic Simulations’ (the publisher) earlier, similar game Computer Bismark. Pursuit of the Graf Spee was among the top rated games in Computer Gaming World’s 1983 game ratings for games from 1982 - 1983. It had an above average user rating of 6.86.

Production History
Pursuit of the Graf Spee was created for the Apple II in 1982. It was created by Joel Billings. Pursuit of the Graf Spee was published by Strategic Simulations, which was especially known for its wargames.
