Suspended

In Suspended, players control the actions of six different robots via computer link-up to the mind. The player’s body is in cryogenic suspension. You are aboard an orbiting space station years in the future, where scientists have finally managed to control the weather. You, the player, wake from cryogenic slumber to find the station in disarray and the weather out of control on the planet’s surface. Without leaving the cryogenic chamber, you must repair the station using the robots you control with your mind.

Impact
Suspended uses the latest in video game technology to control multiple characters simultaneously. Robots you control in the game can be sent on different tasks at the same time. Softline’s May 1983 issue states that Suspended “represents another milestone in the continuing evolution of the interactive computer model”. Suspended ranked number 1 in Softalk’s July 1983 issue’s top five adventure games and 23 in the top thirty of that same issue.

Production History
Suspended was created in 1982. It was created by Michael Berlyn. Suspended was published by Infocom. Infocom was bought by Activision in 1986 and shut down in 1989.
