Minotaur

In Minotaur, you are the Greek hero Theseus, tackling a series of mazes trying to escape the Minotaur. If you see the Minotaur, you can shoot him and then advance to a more difficult maze. There are a total of 32 mazes that vary from game to game. The aim of the game is simply to reach the exit without running into the Minotaur.

Impact
The Softline May 1982 issue compares Minotaur to “real life” stating that Minotaur resembles taking the wrong ramp off a freeway in a strange part of town and trying to find your way back on. Minotaur’s mazes were randomly generated making play different every game.

Production History
Minotaur was created in 1982. It was created by Larry Miller. Minotaur was published by Sirius Software, also known for Space Eggs, and a victim of the “Shakeout”. During the Shakeout in the mid-1980s, games failed because of the failure of companies that made the platforms for the games. Microcomputers and consoles like the Commodore VIC and Atari stopped being made and game makers were left with unsellable games and losses that could not be recouped from the manufacturing of games created for the defunct consoles.
