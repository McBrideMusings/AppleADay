Crisis Mountain

Crisis Mountain is a game of the “Donkey Kong variety” where players defuse nuclear devices planted in caverns beneath a volcano. You must avoid boulders, rocks, and fireballs. Time fuses on the nuclear devices add to the stress of defusing the bombs. Players start with three little men or characters who can gain and lose strength throughout the game.

Quick Start
This game was originally played with a joystick, so to emulate that you move your mouse cursor to the outer edges of the emulator screen. Left click causes your character to jump. Right Click causes him to crawl.

If you ever can't control the game with your keyboard, try clicking on the screen.

Impact
In Crisis Mountain, no two games seem alike. As the Softline January 1983 issue puts it, “you can be getting along just fine and suddenly be devastated in a matter of seconds”. Crisis Mountain ranked 7 in Softline’s March 1983 issue’s top ten arcade games and 20 in its top thirty list.

Production History
Crisis Mountain was created in 1982. It was created by David Schroeder. Crisis Mountain was published by Synergistic Software, which published some of the earliest available games and applications for the Apple II.
