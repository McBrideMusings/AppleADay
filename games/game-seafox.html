Seafox

In Seafox, you control a submarine on a convoy hunt. Your mission is to sink the top tier of ships in the convoy: tankers, freighters, and liners. You must also dodge enemy ships patrolling the depths of the ocean. Described as colorful and enjoyable, Seafox “promises many hours of diversion” (Softalk November 1982).

Impact
Softline states Seafox is evidence of Brøderbund’s golden touch. Softalk’s September 1982 issue ranked Seafox number 27 in its top thirty list. Seafox was among the rated games in Computer Gaming World’s 1983 game ratings for games from 1982 - 1983. However, it had a below average user rating of 5.71.

Production History
Seafox was created by Ed Hobbs in 1982. It was published by Brøderbund Software. Brøderbund, headed by Doug Carlston - who was one a member of the “brotherhood” of game makers in the early 1980s - was purchased by The Learning Company in 1998. However, the brand can still be seen on some original Brøderbund products.
