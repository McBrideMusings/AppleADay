The Dark Crystal

The Dark Crystal is based on Jim Henson's film of the same name. You play as Jen, the last survivor of the Gelfings. You must find a dark crystal and its broken shard, and repair it to end the reign of a tyrannical lord.

Quick Start
Start by loading 1A, and the game will quickly prompt you to load 1B. After that just follow the instructions and load a new dsk/nib as prompted. The Dark Crystal uses the standard object verb adventure game syntax of the era, with similar keywords, including LOOK AROUND, GO NORTH/SOUTH/EAST/WEST, TAKE, etc.

If you ever can't control the game with your keyboard, try clicking on the screen.

Impact
Softline’s May 1983 issue gives the game a positive review, even if it can’t do the same for the movie, stating, “graphic adventures are so often weak in plot, the thin story that failed to serve the movie well is comparatively top-drawer material in the game”. The Dark Crystal ranked number 3 in Softalk’s May 1983 issue’s top five adventure games and 29 in the top thirty of that same issue.

Production History
The Dark Crystal was created in 1983. It was developed by Roberta Williams and published by Sierra On-line. Roberta was the co-founder of Sierra On-line and known for being one of few women in video game development.
