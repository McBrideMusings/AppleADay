Hard Hat Mack

Hard Hat Mack is a game similar to Donkey Kong, where you complete specific objectives in a construction site while avoiding enemies and trying not to run out of time. Your immediate goal is to move girders into the holes in the uncompleted building, then rivet them into place.

Quick Start
This game was originally played with a Joystick, so to emulate that you move your cursor to the edges of the emulator to move your character. Left Click jumps. You'll automatically pick up girders and you should automatically place them in uncompleted walkways.

The disk acquired has a graphical glitch, making your character appear as a distorted image unless jumping or moving in the elevator.

In the July issue of Softline, the high score for Hard Hat Mack was 20,305.

If you ever can't control the game with your keyboard, try clicking on the screen.

Impact
When Hard Hat Mack was released, “the response was overwhelming” according to the July 1983 issue of Softline. It was one of the first games from Electric Arts (better known today as EA games). Hard Hat Mack ranked 17 in Softalk’s August 1983 issue’s top thirty.

Production History
Hard Hat Mack was created by Michael Abbot and Matthew Alexanderin 1983. It was published by Electronic Arts. The company (known today as EA Games) is considered one of the largest players in the industry, often through acquisitions of successful development studios.
