Crossfire

Crossfire was a game made for the “dedicated and persevering gamester”. The January 1982 Softline review described it as challenging and time-consuming where perhaps a “third, supercoordinated appendage” is needed. A lack of a pause button contributes to the game’s difficulty.

Quick Start
In Crossfire, press I,J,K and L to move up, down, left and right and E, S, D and F. Destroy as many enemies as possible and stay alive as long as possible to achive a high score.

In the January issue of Softline, the high score for Crossfire was 241,800.

If you ever can't control the game with your keyboard, try clicking on the screen

Impact
Despite being described as very difficult in Softline, the game is also touted as “a new twist at arcade games with delightfully colorful beasties and smooth animation”. However, the game was not rated in Softalk in January or February of 1982.

Production History
Crossfire was released in 1981. It was created by Jay Sullivan and published by On-line Systems (now and more commonly known as Sierra On-line). Softline stated that Jay Sullivan provided a game that offered hours of challenge and enjoyment.
