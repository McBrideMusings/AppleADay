Repton

In Repton, a player attempts to defend her home world of Repton. Leading a squadron of fighter ships (one at a time), a player fights the hordes of evil Quarriors attempting to invade Repton. Quarriors are resourceful and deadly – only the greatest fighter will prevail!

Impact
Repton is a side scrolling game inspired by Defender, an earlier game from Sirius (the publisher), but adds to the concept. According to the Softline January 1983 issue, Repton is the latest in “ultra-fast-action arcade games” and a “killer game”. Repton is ranked number 9 in the top ten arcade games in Softline’s March 1983 issue.

Production History
Repton was created in 1983. It was created by Dan Thompson and Andrew Kaluzniacki. Repton was published by Sirius Software, a victim of the “Shakeout”. During the Shakeout in the mid-1980s, games failed because of the failure of companies that made the platforms for the games. Microcomputers and consoles like the Commodore VIC and Atari stopped being made and game makers were left with unsellable games and losses that could not be recouped from the manufacturing of games created for the defunct consoles.
